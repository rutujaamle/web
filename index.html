We aim to create a sustainable culture in water management

We at EKATVAM have developed smart water resource management tool, which helps the villages guide, optimize and monitor utilization of water. At the core, the technology includes the EKATVAM unique data analytics algorithm, which aids in strengthening the Decision Support System (DSS) of rural community.
With the help of scientific methodology of our tool we are empowering the Rural community by our three pillars Data procurement, Data analytics and Community participation

Product review

MIDAS-Model of Interactive Decision Assistance Simulator

MIDAS is mobile platform tool which enables decision gram panchayat of village in water managment

  • Water balance will be shown to Gram Panchayat/ WUA’s
  • Showcasing the surface water allotted & the demand of the WUA’s
  • Providing What-if scenarios on demand side interventions
  • Users: Gram Panchayats/ WUA’s

AU - MIDAS

AU MIDAS showcases authority level dashboard which includes data analytics.It helps the user to monitor realtime water managment with bunch of villages and also it helps to implement advisories at authority level whic will aid to take scientific decision.

  • Generating maps on land use, recharge priority, and water body locations
  • Showcasing groundwater, crop water and domestic trends for every village
  • Listing possible village-specific interventions based on water balance/li>
  • Users: Village Coordinator/Supervisor & Irrigation Department

Our Services

Data trainagulation & automation

Quicker access to localized data and periodic generation of near real-time water balance for any village

Enabling Foresightedness

Enabling foresightedness in the community by projecting probable impact of each intervention on the on-ground water scenario

Enabling Community Participation

Fast-tracking the effort in enabling community discussion on water management through visualizations and simulations of water scenario in their village

Better Visualization

Better visualization and identification of the on-ground needs through maps and graphs for every village, thus enabling a scientific approach in designing interventions
